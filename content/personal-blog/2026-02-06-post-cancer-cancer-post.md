## üò¥ First Symptoms

A little background: I live in Denver, CO with my (now) husband Liam & our french bulldog, Jack (the light of our lives). In February of 2024, I had just finished a programming bootcamp in Denver as I was changing careers. About a month into my job search, I could tell that *something* was starting to feel different. I began to notice that concentrating on tasks was slightly more difficult  and I started to feel general fatigue during the day, usually after noon-ish. Sometimes the symptoms were very minor, basically unnoticeable, and sometimes I could barely get any kind of work done. It became especially frustrating when, after a few years of finally getting into a good exercise routine, I could feel myself starting to slip out of it - even just slightly.  

For me, it was really easy to chalk this up to general feelings of depression in the job search ‚Äì I kept telling myself that things would get better once I had a job and didn't spend so much time indoors. After I let this bother me for long enough, I had a close friend recommend that I at least make a doctor's appointment to see if I could get prescribed something for depression that might, in turn, help with the general tiredness I was feeling. 

I agreed, and made an appointment with a new PCP. 

During my first appointment with this doctor in August of 2024, I mentioned the general tiredness and difficulty concentrating I'd been experiencing, and was prescribed an antidepressant. But before I left, I also inquired to this doctor about getting a general blood test done, something I'd been especially sensitive toward after a very close friend of mine lost her husband to [cholangiocarcinoma, also known as bile duct cancer](https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/conditions-and-diseases/bile-duct-cancer-cholangiocarcinoma). I shared this story with my doctor, and despite my tiredness symptoms, she assured me that a blood panel wouldn't be necessary, and that I was generally pretty healthy. While somewhat annoyed, I believed what she said and I left that appointment without any real follow ups. 

I now know that during this appointment in August of 2024, I already had a tumor growing in my colon, where it would continue growing for *at least* another year.

Just to take a pause here because this is one of those absolutely insane moments that I feel like needs reiterating: the main reason I asked for a blood test, at least at this first appointment, was because my friend's husband had learned of his cancer that way, and I just ‚Äúwanted to check‚Äù, and while I was asking about this (and getting rejected), I *had a tumor*. 

Um. That is NUTS. Anyways. Moving on.

## üçÄ Lucky Me! (Part 1 out of 4,815,162,342)

The newest blood panel I did came back mostly normal (which itself is scary because I personally just believed for no particular reason that blood tests would figure out all my problems). The only thing that came back abnormal was my iron levels, and I was instructed to begin taking an iron supplement. 

However, at my appointment with my new (lifesaving) PCP in August of 2025, she immediately told me to stop taking the iron supplement. Fun little fact I learned: [it's more common for women to have anemia (iron deficiency) than men due to menstrual cycles](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/21934611/). With men, it can be cause for concern, or... it can also be fine. In my case, it was not fine. 

At this appointment, I imagine reality being violently torn in half, and I am teetering on the edge between both of them: in one reality, my doctor dismisses the symptoms and the path I go down is much darker that the one I‚Äôve been down. But for whatever reason, whether it be luck, or god, or privilege, or fate, or something else entirely, I get to take the path where my doctor asks: ‚Äúany changes in bowel movements recently?‚Äù  

What had changed was this (look away now if you must!): 

* I found myself needing to use the bathroom MUCH more frequently ‚Äì like, literally, 10-15+ times a day, usually more so in the morning. Does that sound crazy? Because yeah, agree.
* Despite each time feeling urgent, only small amounts would come out, and there was usually blood (bright red). 
* I felt a little physical discomfort in my abdomen, but not much - and I honestly attributed it to the coffee I was drinking (I absolutely never tried to stop drinking coffee). 

At this moment, after sharing what had changed with my PCP, my doctor immediately referred me to get a colonoscopy.
